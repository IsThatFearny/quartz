# Text Elements
What is knowledge? - Definitions ^VuIvgOcF

A priori: Can be known prior to
sense experience

A Posteriori: Can only be known
after sense experience

Analytical statement: True by definition,
and in all possible worlds

Synthetic statement: True because of
how the world is; could be different in
other possible worlds

Ability knowledge: Something that we have
learnt how to do, e.g. riding a bike. It is
subconscious and difficult to articulate

 ^3kAj9btR

Acquaintance knowledge: Knowledge that is
gained through experience and becoming
acquainted with something, e.g. knowing Oxford,
or your best friend

Proposition: What is expressed by a declarative
statement

Propositional knowledge: Knowledge that we know
to be true or false. Can be a priori

Necessary condition: A condition which must be present
for a definition to be accurate

Sufficient condition: A condition which fulfils the criteria
for a definition without need for further conditions ^9XIOjjfI
