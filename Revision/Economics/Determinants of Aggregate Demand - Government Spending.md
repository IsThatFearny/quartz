Real output increases when there are periods of economic growth (Recovery). The boom is when economic growth
which could be inflationary or unsustainable. During recessions, real output falls, and there is negative growth.
Governments might increase spending to try and stimulate the economy. This will have to be financed somehow,
and may cause an increase in the budget deficit. During periods of growth, governments may receive more tax
revenue since consumers will be spending and earning more. They may therefore decide to spend less.

Fiscal Policy: Governments change spending and taxation to influence the economy. They might spend on public
and merit goods, and welfare payments. Discretionary fiscal policy is a policy which is implemented through
one-off policy changes. Automatic stabilisers are policies which offset fluctuations in the economy, which are
triggered without government intervention. The government might use expansionary fiscal policy during periods
of decline, which involves increasing spending and reducing taxes. During periods of growth, contractionary fiscal
policy may be used by decreasing expenditure and increasing taxes. ^8jBqzgPA

The Trade/Business Cycle: The stage of economic growth that the economy is in. The economy goes through
periods of booms and busts: ^CzY4qgOG
