Consumer Prices Index/Retail Prices Index ^JPBHmXDP

CPI and RPI are the measures of inflation in the UK. The CPI
measures household purchasing power using the Family Expenditure
Survey. From this, a basket of goods is created, with different items
weighted depending on the proportion of income spent on them.
The changes of price in these commonly bought goods illustrates the general
change in the price level. RPI is the same, but takes into account housing costs. ^0IMEJkzU

Productivity ^Y9FJZxjI

Productivity is the output per worker per period of time.

units of input. Productivity in the UK is measured by output per hour. ^hCps5Ncf

Balance of Payments ^U9KGJkyG

The current account is comprised of exports and imports.

Real GDP per capita is Real GDP divided by the population

Unemployment can be difficult to measure (hidden statistics and the like).
