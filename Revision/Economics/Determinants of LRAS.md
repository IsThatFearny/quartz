Determinants of LRAS ^cm3TzQlQ

It is assumed that the LRAS curve is vertical. This view suggests
that output is fixed at each level. All factors of production in the 
economy are fully employed in the long run. This means that changing 
AD, such as from AD1 to AD2, only makes a change in the price level 
(from P1 to P2), and it will not change national output (Real GDP). 
The position of the vertical LRAS curve represents the normal 
capacity level of output of the economy. ^NB7R2h2Z

Factors influencing the LRAS ^PPvmbWSt

Shifts in the LRAS curve are equivalent to shifts in the PPF.

Technological advances: If more money is spent on improving technology, the economy can 
produce goods in larger volumes or improve their quality.

Changes in relative productivity: A more productive labour and capital input will produce a 
larger quantity of output with the same quantity of input.

Changes in education/skills: This improves the quality of human capital, so it is more productive.

Changes in government regulations: Government regulation could limit how productive and efficient 
a firm can be if it is excessive. Known as 'Red Tape'.

Demographic Changes and migration: If there is net inward migration and the majority of the 
population is of working age, the size of the labour force is going to be significant, which means 
the economy can increase its output..

Competition policy: A more competitive market encourages firms to be more efficient and more 
productive, so that they are not driven out of business. Governments can use effective competition 
policy to stimulate this in the economy.
