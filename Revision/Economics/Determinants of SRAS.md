Determinants of SRAS ^HH2ePjpa

The SRAS curve shifts when there are changes in the conditions of supply. The price level and production
costs are the main determinants of SRAS.

The cost of employment might change. If costs increase, supply will shift inwards from SRAS 1 to SRAS 3.

The cost of other inputs is also a factor. A stronger currency reduces the price of imports, so imported
products will be cheaper. This would shift the AS curve outwards, from SRAS1 to SRAS2.

Government regulation or intervention, such as environmental laws or green taxes and business regulation,
is also important. Business regulation is sometimes called 'red tape'.

There could be a net outward migration of workers, which causes a 'brain drain' on the domestic economy
as skilled workers move elsewhere.
