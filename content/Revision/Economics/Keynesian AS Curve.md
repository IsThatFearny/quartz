# Text Elements
The Keynesian AS Curve ^tbDKqePs

The Keynesian view suggests that the price level in the economy is fixed until resources are fully employed.
The horizontal section shows the output and price level when resources are not fully employed; there is
spare capacity in the economy. The vertical section is when resources are fully employed. Over the spare
capacity section, output can be increased (AD1 to AD2) without affecting the price level (stays at P1). In
other words, output changes are not inflationary. Once resources are fully employed, an increase in output
(AD3 to AD4) will be inflationary (price level increases from P2 to P3) ^7RzsrxuL
