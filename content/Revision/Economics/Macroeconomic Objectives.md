Economic Growth ^jvDk5qLk

Governments aim to have long-term, sustainable economic growth.
Short-term growth is acceptable, but this will likely not have as

Low Unemployment ^5mdzQyuY

Governments aim to come as close to full employment as possible.
To account for frictional unemployment (unemployment whilst moving
between jobs), most governments aim for a rate of approx. 3%.
It is also important that the labour force are employed in productive
work. The UK's unemployment target is 2%. ^fCFoGa7y

Low/Stable Inflation ^Fly3f87c

The UK's inflation target is 2%, the aim of which is to
provide price stability for firms and consumers. If there 
is confidence that prices will remain stable, then firms and
consumers are able to make long-run economic decisions. ^7A1DkHcS

Stable current account balance ^fLtOZuqw

Governments aim for the current account to be satisfactory,
i.e. there is no large deficit. The 'satisfactory' position is
usually close to the equilibrium. A balanced current account is
desirable because it allows the country to sustainably finance
the current account, which is important to help long-term growth ^MxEy9Cuu

Additional Macroeconomic Objectives ^F2Y0EXq1

Balanced Government Budget ^MFRsAwnp

Maintaining control of state borrowing prevents the national debt
from increasing to an unmanageable amount. This improves the
government's credit, and makes repayment easier ^ar1vuwi8

Greater Income Equality ^HtF34A3T

between rich and poor is kept to a minimum. This is generally associated
with a fairer and more equal society ^B9HdLNRq
