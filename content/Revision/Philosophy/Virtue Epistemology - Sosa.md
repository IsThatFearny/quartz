The arrow hits the target; the belief is true

  The archer is skilled; the believer is intellectually virtuous

 ^KoVYV7LP

The arrow hits the target because of the archer's skill;
the belief is true because of the believer's intellectual virtues ^i6hZWUIw

The third condition, Aptness, differentiates Sosa's account from those criticised by Zagzebski.
Because Sosa argues that a belief has to be true as a direct consequences of a virtuous
act, he bridges the gap between truth and the third condition, and so rules out Gettier cases.  ^88Ay8tAz
