# Text Elements
Responses to Illusion ^mZCWLgg8

Direct Realism gives the same reply as it does to perceptual variation. The pencil has
the property of 'looking crooked'. There is a difference between 'being crooked' and 
'looking crooked'. The two properties can come apart. Therefore, in illusions, we perceive
the 'looks' properties of physical objects, and these 'looks' properties don't match the 
'is' properties of the object. But we still directly perceive physical objects 
and their properties. ^S8yiy4bs
